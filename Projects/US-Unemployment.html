Unemployment in America – A Data-Driven Overview

This dashboard provides a comprehensive overview of unemployment in the United States as of May 2025, integrating national, state-level, and metro-area data to uncover both surface-level trends and deeper structural patterns.

While the national unemployment rate appears stable, disaggregating the data reveals significant disparities across states and urban regions. Cities such as New York, Los Angeles, and Washington D.C. show unique labor dynamics that differ markedly from their surrounding states, reflecting the influence of industry concentration, population density, and local policy environments.

By combining short-term (MoM) and long-term (YoY) variations with volume-based indicators, this dashboard goes beyond static metrics to support more informed decision-making.

The final section—focused on structural correlations—demonstrates that unemployment rates are not simply a function of population size or labor force scale. Instead, contextual factors play a critical role in shaping labor outcomes across the country.
